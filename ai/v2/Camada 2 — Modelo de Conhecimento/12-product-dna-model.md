# Specforge V2 Product DNA Model

## Status

Accepted

## Purpose

This document defines the Product DNA Model used by Specforge.

The Product DNA Model transforms product identity into a structured and operational knowledge object.

Its purpose is to ensure that project identity remains durable, discoverable and enforceable throughout the lifecycle of a project.

The Product DNA is one of the highest-level knowledge objects within Specforge.

All lower-level artifacts should align with it.

---

# Introduction

Many projects define:

- requirements
- features
- architecture

Few projects define:

- identity
- philosophy
- decision-making rules

As projects evolve, these elements often become implicit.

The result is gradual product drift.

Specforge introduces Product DNA to preserve project identity as a first-class knowledge asset.

---

# Definition

## Product DNA

Product DNA is the structured representation of a project's identity.

It answers:

- Who are we?
- Why do we exist?
- How do we make decisions?
- What should we never become?

Product DNA is not a specification.
Product DNA is not a roadmap.
Product DNA is not a feature list.

Product DNA is project identity.

- guide decisions

---

# Product DNA Structure

Every Product DNA should contain:

## Mission

Why does this product exist?

## Vision

What future are we trying to create?

## Problem Statement

What problem justifies this product?

## Target Audience

Who are we building for?

## Core Beliefs

Examples:

- Knowledge is more valuable than prompts.
- Better context produces better AI outcomes.

## Core Principles

Examples:

- Knowledge First
- Preserve Rationale
- Transparency

## Constraints

Examples:

- BYOK
- Open Source
- Free Forever

## Non-Goals

Examples:

- Project Management
- Sprint Planning
- Ticket Tracking

## Product Personality

Examples:

- Thoughtful
- Structured
- Transparent
- Practical

## Decision Filters

Examples:

- Does this improve knowledge preservation?
- Does this improve context quality?

# Product DNA Validation

A Product DNA is healthy when:

- mission is clear
- audience is clear
- principles are explicit
- constraints are explicit
- non-goals are documented
- decision filters exist

---

# Product DNA Quality Evaluation

## Clarity

Can a new developer understand it?

## Consistency

Does it avoid contradictions?

## Durability

Will it remain useful in one year?

## Actionability

Can it guide decisions?

## Alignment

Do generated artifacts follow it?

---

# Product DNA Drift

Product DNA Drift occurs when artifacts begin conflicting with Product DNA.

Examples:

- Feature Drift
- Audience Drift
- Philosophy Drift
- Scope Drift

---

# Drift Detection

Future versions of Specforge should detect:

- conflicting requirements
- conflicting decisions
- conflicting roadmaps
- conflicting specifications

against Product DNA.

---

# Product DNA Preservation Rules

Always preserve:

- mission
- vision
- audience
- principles
- constraints
- non-goals
- decision filters

---

# AI Responsibilities

AI systems using Specforge should:

- consult Product DNA before generating artifacts
- identify conflicts with Product DNA
- explain alignment decisions
- recommend Product DNA updates when necessary

AI should never silently violate Product DNA.

---

# Future Evolution

Future versions may introduce:

- Product DNA scoring
- Product DNA health metrics

- automated consistency checks

---

# Product DNA as a Source of Truth

Product DNA is not merely documentation.

It is an operational knowledge object.

Its purpose is to continuously influence future decisions.

Product DNA should remain active knowledge rather than archived knowledge.

---

# Final Statement

Requirements describe what should be built.

Specifications describe how it should be built.

Decisions describe what was chosen.

Product DNA describes who the project is.

Without Product DNA, projects gradually lose identity.

Specforge preserves identity as a first-class knowledge asset.
