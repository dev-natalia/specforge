# Specforge V2 Knowledge Preservation

## Status

Accepted

## Purpose

This document defines the Knowledge Preservation model used by Specforge.

Knowledge Preservation is responsible for ensuring that critical project knowledge survives context compression, provider changes, team changes and project evolution.

Its purpose is to transform fragile conversational knowledge into durable project assets.

Knowledge Preservation protects project intelligence from loss.

---

# Introduction

Projects accumulate knowledge continuously.

Examples:

- discoveries
- decisions
- constraints
- tradeoffs
- architectural rationale
- business understanding

Most of this knowledge is lost.

Common causes:

- forgotten conversations
- staff turnover
- provider changes
- incomplete documentation
- context window limitations

Knowledge Preservation exists to prevent this loss.

---

# Definition

## Knowledge Preservation

Knowledge Preservation is the process of identifying, storing, protecting and evolving project knowledge over time.

A preservation strategy answers:

- What knowledge is valuable?
- What knowledge must be retained?
- What knowledge may be archived?
- How is knowledge recovered?

Preservation focuses on long-term usefulness.

# Preservation Priorities

## Critical Knowledge

Must never be lost.

Examples:

- major decisions
- architecture rationale
- security rationale
- product DNA

Highest preservation priority.

## Important Knowledge

Strong preservation priority.

Examples:

- discoveries
- constraints
- design rationale

## Reference Knowledge

Useful but non-critical.

## Archive Knowledge

Preserved for recovery purposes.

Lowest priority.

# Knowledge Capture Rules

Knowledge should be captured when:

- A Discovery Is Validated
- A Decision Is Approved
- A Constraint Is Identified
- A Tradeoff Is Accepted
- A Repeated Explanation Emerges

Repeated explanations indicate important knowledge.

---

# Preservation Rules

The following information should never be discarded:

- Product DNA
- Core Principles
- Approved Decisions
- Architectural Rationale
- Security Rationale
- Business Constraints
- Domain Terminology

---

# Knowledge Evolution

Knowledge is not static.

Knowledge should support:

Preserve
↓
Review
↓
Update
↓
Version
↓
Preserve Again

Preservation does not imply immutability.

---

# Versioning Requirements

Knowledge artifacts should support:

- Version History
- Change Reasoning
- Superseded Knowledge
- Active Knowledge

Knowledge evolution should remain explainable.

---

# Recovery Requirements

Preserved knowledge should be recoverable.

Recovery should answer:

- Why was this decision made?
- What alternatives were considered?
- Which constraints existed?
- What changed?

Recovery should be fast and reliable.

## Conversation Reliance

Important knowledge exists only in chat history.

## Decision Loss

## Compression Loss

Knowledge scattered across unrelated locations.
